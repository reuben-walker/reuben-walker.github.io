Bivariate Choropleth Analysis of Break-Ins vs Income Levels

Simon Fraser University, Quantitative Geography
April 2018

(Click to enlarge)

Project Description:


This map shows the correlation between income levels and percent share of all residential break & enter crimes in each census dissemination area in Vancouver. Multiple regression analysis was used to examine the correlation between percentage of men, percentage of women, income level, and residential break & enter crimes for each area. The analysis revealed that gender did not have a statistically significant correlation with proportion of break & enter crimes. However, income level did have a correlation. Therefore, only the correlation between income level and break & enter crimes was mapped using the bivariate choropleth method.

Software Used:

  • Esri ArcMap

Data Sources:

  • City of Vancouver Open Data Portal: City boundaries, census dissemination areas, gender, crime, and income statistics
